The chart below shows what happened during the study.
Before the participants got study treatment:

- 1 visit
- The study doctors checked the health of the participants to check their heart health. They also used an electrocardiogram (ECG) to make sure they could join the study.
- They did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- If necessary, they used surgery to take a kidney biopsy.
- They took blood and urine samples.
- They checked how well the participants were able to do their usual daily activities through x-ray scans of their chests.

During the participants' study treatment:

- At least 13 visits
- The study doctors did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- They took blood and urine samples.
- The participants received IV infusions of their study drug once every 4 weeks for 52 weeks.
- If they had a response to their study drug, they could receive the same study drug for another 52 weeks.
- They took standard of care treatment as tablets by mouth every day.
- They checked how well the participants were able to do their usual daily activities.

After the participants got study treatment:

- 2 visits
- The study doctors did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- They took blood and urine samples.
- They checked how well the participants were able to do their usual daily activities.
