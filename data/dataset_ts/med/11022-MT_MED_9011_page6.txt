What happened during the study?

Each participant was in the study for about 9 months. But, the entire study took just over 1 year to finish. The study started in May 2019 and ended in June 2020.

Before the participants got study treatment:

- 1 visit
- The study doctors checked the health of the participants to checked the participants' lung health and make sure they could join the study.
- The study doctors measured the amount of air the participants could breathe out.
- The study doctors did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- The study doctors took blood and urine samples.
- The study doctors checked the participants' heart health using an electrocardiogram, also called an ECG.
- The participants answered questionnaires about their symptoms.

Up to 2 weeks while the participants got study treatment:

- 7 visits
- The study doctors did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- The study doctors supervised the participant or caregiver injecting the study drug at the study site for the next 2 doses and final dose.
- The study doctors checked the participants' lung health and measured the amount of air they could breathe out.
- The participants answered questionnaires on if the injection was successful and about their symptoms.
- The study doctors took blood and urine samples.
- The participants or caregivers injected 2-3 doses of the study drug at the study site and 2 doses at home.

After the participants got study treatment:

- 2 visits
- The study doctors did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- The study doctors took blood and urine samples.
