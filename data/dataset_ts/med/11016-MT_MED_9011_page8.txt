What happened during this study?

Each of the participants was in the study for up to just over 22 weeks. But, the entire study took 15 months to finish.

The study started in April 2019 and ended in July 2020.

Some participants were still in the study when the COVID-19 pandemic began. For these participants, some visits were done through phone calls rather than in person.

Before the participants took study treatment, the study doctors:

- Checked the health of the participants to make sure they could join the study.
- Answered questionnaires about the participants' symptoms.
- Did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- Took blood and urine samples.

Some participants also:

- Checked the participants' heart health using an electrocardiogram (ECG).
- Wore a device that measured their activity levels.

This part of the study took 2 weeks.

While the participants took study treatment, the study doctors:

- Did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medications and any medical problems.
- Took blood and urine samples.
- Answered questionnaires about their symptoms.
- Did the 6MWD test (6-minute walk distance test).

Some participants also:

- Wore a device that measured their activity levels.

This part of the study took 16 weeks.
