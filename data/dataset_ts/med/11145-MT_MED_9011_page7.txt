What happened during this study?
The participants were in the study until their cancer got worse, the study doctors thought they should stop study treatment, or they left for another reason. The entire study took about 2 years to finish. The study started in August 2019 and ended in June 2021.

Before the participants received study treatment
1 visit
The study doctors:
- Checked the health of the participants 
- Took pictures of the participants' tumors
- Ensured they could join the study using CT, MRI, PET, or bone scans
- Did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medical problems
- Checked the participants' heart health using an electrocardiogram (ECG)
- Answered surveys about their symptoms and ability to perform daily activities
- Took blood and urine samples

Up to 28 days
While the participants were receiving study treatment
1 to 8 visits a month
The study doctors:
- Did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medical problems
- Checked the participants' heart health using an electrocardiogram (ECG)
- Answered surveys about their symptoms and ability to perform daily activities
- Took blood and urine samples

The participants:
- Received their study treatment
- Took pictures of the participants' tumors using CT, MRI, PET, or bone scans
- Answered surveys about their symptoms and ability to perform daily activities
- Took blood and urine samples

Until the cancer got worse
After the participants received study treatment
1 or more visits
At some visits, the study doctors:
- Did a physical exam and asked about the participants' medical problems
- Took pictures of the participants' tumors using CT, MRI, PET, or bone scans
- Checked the participants' heart health using an electrocardiogram (ECG)
- Took blood and urine samples

The participants:
- Answered surveys about their symptoms and ability to perform daily activities
- Took blood and urine samples

Until the end of the study
