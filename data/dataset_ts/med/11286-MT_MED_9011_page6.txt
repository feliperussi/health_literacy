What medical problems happened during the study?
This section is a summary of the medical problems the participants had during the study that the study doctors thought might be related to the study drug. These medical problems are called "adverse reactions". An adverse reaction is considered "serious" when it is life-threatening, causes lasting problems, or requires hospital care.

These adverse reactions may or may not be caused by the study drug. A lot of research is needed to know whether a drug causes an adverse reaction. These adverse reactions have been, and will continue to be, reviewed together with all of the available data for the study drug.

The websites listed at the end of this summary may have other information about adverse reactions or other medical problems that happened during this study.

Did any adverse reactions happen during this study?
There were 33.3% of participants who had adverse reactions during this study. This was 4 out of 12 participants.
None of the participants left this study due to adverse reactions.

What serious adverse reactions happened during this study?
None of the participants had serious adverse reactions during this study.
None of the participants died due to serious adverse reactions during this study.
