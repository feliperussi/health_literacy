Clinical Study Results
What medical problems did the participants have
during the study?
This section is a summary of the adverse events the participants had during the
study that the study doctors thought might be related to the study treatment. These
adverse events are called “adverse reactions”. An adverse reaction is considered
“serious” when it is life-threatening, causes lasting problems, or requires hospital
care.
Adverse reactions may or may not be caused by the study treatment. A lot of
research is needed to know whether a treatment causes an adverse reaction.
The websites listed at the end of this summary may have more information about
the adverse reactions that happened during this study.
How many participants had serious adverse reactions?
None of the participants had serious adverse reactions during the study.
How many participants had adverse reactions?
There were 10.9% of participants who had adverse reactions during the study. This
was 7 out of 64 participants.
